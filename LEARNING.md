## 18.04.2026 - Computer Hardware
**Source:** [IBM: Hardware Topics](https://www.ibm.com/think/topics/hardware)

* **CPU** - The brain that controls everything.
* **RAM** - Temporary storage during the session.
* **SSD/HDD** - Permanent storage.
* **I/O Devices** - Everything you plug in, like mice, keyboards, etc.

## 19.04.2026 - Internet Connectivity
**Source:** [NordVPN: Connection Types](https://nordvpn.com/blog/network-connection-types/)

* **Wired Connections** - Cables like Ethernet (high stability) and DSL (phone lines). Faster and more reliable.
* **Wireless Connections** - Wi-Fi, Cellular (4G/5G), and Bluetooth. High mobility but prone to interference.
* **Broadband** - High-speed, "always-on" internet connection with high bandwidth.

## 20.04.2026 - Near Field Communication (NFC)
**Source:** [Spiceworks: NFC Networking](https://www.spiceworks.com/networking/what-is-near-field-communication/)

* **Definition** - A short-range wireless technology (PAN) for data transfer within 4-10 cm. 
* **Operating Modes**:
    * **Active** - Both devices are powered (e.g., phone to phone).
    * **Passive** - One device powers the other via electromagnetic induction (e.g., phone to NFC tag).
* **Key Use Cases** - Contactless payments (Apple/Google Pay), smart keys, and instant pairing.
* **Security Note** - Susceptible to eavesdropping and **Relay Attacks** despite the short range.
